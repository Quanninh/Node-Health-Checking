\begin{abstract}
  Traditional distributed systems often rely on centralized heartbeat-based mechanisms to monitor node
  health and maintain membership information.
  While such approaches simplify coordination and system management, they introduce several limitations,
  including false-positive failure detections, single points of failure, and scalability bottlenecks.
  This study addresses the challenge of designing a decentralized overlay network that mitigates these
  limitations while maintaining connectivity and resilience.
  To achieve this objective, we propose a decentralized membership management framework composed of three
  key components.
  New nodes are integrated into the overlay through FINDING NEMO, a neighbor discovery algorithm that
  employs a join-and-evict strategy to distribute connections while preserving bounded network density and
  randomness in topology formation.
  Once nodes become members of the network, a hybrid failure detection scheme combining the SWIM protocol
  and phi-accrual failure detection continuously monitors node health with improved accuracy and adaptability.
  Upon detecting peer failures that may leave portions of the overlay under-connected, CHUALANH, a
  self-healing rewiring mechanism, coordinates topology repairs among deficient and sufficiently connected
  nodes to restore connectivity.
  Experimental evaluation demonstrates success rates consistently above 90\% in four out of five evaluation phases, while the remaining phase achieves satisfactory performance under more challenging conditions.
  These results indicate that the proposed framework provides a resilient and balanced decentralized overlay through adaptive failure detection, structured neighbor discovery, and coordinated rewiring.
  Nevertheless, further large-scale experimentation is required to evaluate its
  performance under higher levels of churn and network scale.
\end{abstract}
